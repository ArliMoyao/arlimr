WELLESLEY SUSTAINIBILITY PROPOSAL

This application solution offers students an alternative to current Wellesley Facebook group for students to buy, sell, and donate items within the Wellesley community.

Role UX/UI Designer, Web Developer, Researcher
Team Solo Project (MIT Media Lab)
Tools HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Firebase, Figma
Timeline Summer 2023

THE PROBLEM AND THE OPPORTUNITY

How can I create sustainability more accessible and efficient to implement at Wellesley College?

Sustainability at Wellesley College is difficult to implement due to the inaccessibility and inefficiency of current second-hand platforms like Wellesley Free and For Sale. Students interested in sustainable living lack access to user-friendly, well-organized tools for buying and selling second-hand items, making it challenging to engage in eco-friendly practices on campus.

We realized that many students at Wellesley would like to lead a more sustainable lifestyle, however face many practical barriers. For example, a more sustainable solution often is too time intensive or potentially more costly. We also realized many students did not know about the existing Wellesley sustainability resources. Through the iterative human centered design process, we created Sage to bridge that gap. Sage's two key functionalities are the following:

  • Acting as place where Wellesley community members can buy and sell items in a streamlined process
  • Acting as a platform for sustainability related events on campus to increase community sustainability interest
Main source to buy or sell items on campus

Main pain points discussed

Poor User Experience on Facebook

Users strongly dislike the Facebook interface, describing it as frustrating and unpleasant, which discourages them from using the Wellesley Free and For Sale group.

Fragmented Buying/Selling Timelines

There is no consistent pattern for when students prefer to buy or sell items—some do so at the beginning, others at the end of the semester, or during breaks—making it hard to match buyers and sellers.

Preference for Convenience

Despite the availability of local options, most students still find Amazon the most convenient for shopping, likely due to ease of use, reliability, and consistent availability.

IDEATION

Creating initial design goals and feedback

1. Identifying the Design Goals

Our vision for the application is guided by three core design adventures:

  • Effortless Discovery: Seamlessly search and explore a well-organized marketplace to find exactly what you need, when you need it.
  • Community Connections: Dive into campus events and sustainability sales—your go-to hub for buying, selling, and learning together.
  • Streamlined Experience: Enjoy a fast, intuitive process for listing, browsing, and exchanging items—making sustainable living simple and time-saving.

3. Design Directions

In this design, the user navigates to the home section of the app where they have the option of scrolling through, or directly searching for the item of interest. They search their item instead, and are shown items near them, as well as other items that can require travel. They can also filter through the results, but clicks on the first result, and decides to buy it.

In this design, the user navigates through the home section, and can demonstrate interest in events where they can buy items. They can click on the events tab located on the sidebar, and have the option of searching through events or clicking on the more recent events. They click over the first event, and are interested in attending. The events page showcases the organizer, location, and description. Once they indicate their interest, they are able to add it to their calendar.

4. Peer Feedback

After sharing our design ideas with classmates, we found that each design had distinct strengths and weaknesses. Design 1 prioritized efficiency and a familiar shopping experience but lacked community engagement and sustainability focus, while Design 2 emphasized community and sustainability but included more complex functionality that could affect user clarity.

ITERATION DESIGN

Identifying Features through Iterations and Prototypes

We created a wireframe prototype utilizing Figma that contained all the main features of our application, such as accessing events, or buying an item. In this step, we also conducted four user tests after our initial wireframe prototype, where one of the features "Event Host Controls" according to some of our user tests was difficult to find due to the small font size, and inconvenient location in our app. This was only challenge many of the users felt needed fixing, but otherwise other aspects of the wireframe design was easy to navigate through.

Step 4: High Fidelity Prototype

In this next part of our prototype design, we focused on the usability and graphic design of our implementation. First, we added some new pages to increase the number of features of our app. Specifically, we built a “Create a New Event” page as well as improved the usability of our “Profile” page by allowing users to see their listings, likes, and sold items. We also recharted some of the flows in our wireframe prototype to ensure smooth and easy navigation. To improve our graphic design, we added colors, fonts, and a custom-designed logo to our app layout.

Step 5: Final Prototype

Our final prototype was created after going through the iterative design process several times, keeping in mind human centered design principles.

  • Satisfaction: Reflected overall user experience (noting possible bias).

Key Findings
  • Event Host Controls remain hard to find, causing most errors and lowest satisfaction.
  • Event Registration is fast, easy, and highly rated by users.
  • App aesthetics and G-Cal integration were praised for intuitiveness and uniqueness.
Improvements
  • Moved and emphasized the 'Event Host Controls' button for better visibility.
  • Plan to add an FAQ page for common confusions.
  • Will introduce a monthly calendar icon for event tracking on user profiles.
Final Takeaway

We achieved our goal: designing a prototype that helps users live more sustainably on campus and fosters community through peer events. Future improvements will focus on clarity, user support, and enhanced event features.
